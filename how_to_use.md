# How to Use AdKrity Lite

Welcome to **AdKrity Lite**, your all-in-one React Native app built with Expo and TypeScript for creating, managing, and tracking ads. This guide will walk you through the setup process and explain how to use the core features of the app.

---
## 1. Getting Started

### Prerequisites
Before running the app, ensure you have the following installed on your machine:
- **Node.js** (v18 or higher recommended)
- **npm** or **Yarn**
- **Expo Go** app installed on your physical mobile device (available on iOS App Store and Google Play Store), OR an iOS Simulator / Android Emulator set up on your machine.

### Installation & Setup

1. **Open your terminal** and navigate to the project root directory (`biz499-main`).
2. **Install dependencies** by running:
   ```bash
   npm install
   ```
3. **Start the Expo development server**:
   ```bash
   npx expo start
   ```

### Running the App
Once the Expo server is running, you will see a QR code in your terminal and browser.
- **Physical Device**: Open the Expo Go app and scan the QR code.
- **iOS Simulator**: Press `i` in the terminal to launch the app on the simulator.
- **Android Emulator**: Press `a` in the terminal to launch the app on the emulator.

---

## 2. Navigating the App

### Onboarding
When you launch the app for the very first time, you will be greeted by a series of swipeable introductory screens. These screens highlight the main benefits and features of the app. Swipe through them to get familiar with what biz499 can do for your business.

### Authentication
After onboarding, you will land on the Authentication flow:
- **Phone & OTP Login**: For now, authentication is handled securely via your phone number and an OTP (One-Time Password).
- **Facebook Integration**: After logging in, you will have the option to connect your Facebook account and manage your Facebook Pages directly from the app.

---

## 3. Core Features

biz499 is organized into several key modules, accessible via the navigation system.

### Dashboard / Home
Your central hub for tracking ad performance. Here you can:
- View high-level statistics and metrics for your ongoing campaigns.
- See a list of your most recently created ads.
- Get a quick snapshot of your account's overall health and engagement.

### Business Profile
Manage the specifics of the business you are promoting. Update your business name, contact information, and branding details to ensure your ads are accurately represented.

### Create Ad Wizard
A step-by-step, intuitive multi-step form to generate new ads.
- **Step 1**: Choose your ad type and objective.
- **Step 2**: Fill in your ad copy (Headline, Description).
- **Step 3**: Upload or select images using the built-in image picker.
- **Step 4**: Review your ad and publish/save it.

### Managing Ads
Navigate to the Ads section to see all the campaigns you've created.
- Filter and search through your active, paused, or completed ads.
- View detailed performance metrics for individual ad campaigns.

### Leads Management
Track the leads generated by your ads. This section will automatically fetch leads from your connected Facebook account, and you can also add leads manually. It allows you to view customer inquiries, contact details, and follow up with potential clients directly from your app.

### Designs & News
- **Designs**: Browse and manage your saved ad creatives and templates.
- **News**: Stay updated with the latest updates, tips, and industry news directly within the app.

### Profile & Settings
Tap on your profile icon to access user settings.
- Edit your personal information.
- Configure app preferences.
- Securely log out of your session.

---

## 4. Technical Notes

- **Persistence**: Your user session, created ads, and preferences are saved locally on your device using `AsyncStorage` and `Zustand`. You won't lose your data when you close the app.
- **Styling**: The app uses `NativeWind`, allowing for a beautiful and consistent UI powered by Tailwind CSS classes.

## Need Help?
If you encounter any issues while running the app:
1. Try clearing the Expo cache by running `npx expo start -c`.
2. Delete the `node_modules` folder and `package-lock.json`, then run `npm install` again.
3. Ensure your Expo Go app on your phone is updated to the latest version.
